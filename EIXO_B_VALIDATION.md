## Abstract Claim Audit

### 1. Cohen's d = 1.84

Status: **not confirmed**

- Poster claim: `d = 1.84`
- Observed comparison: normative vs anxious trajectory-level `C_ent` variance
- Observed Cohen's d: `-0.266`
- 95% bootstrap CI: `[-0.290, -0.242]`
- Welch p-value: `7.17e-78`

Interpretation:

- The effect is real in the statistical sense, but it goes in the opposite direction from the poster framing.
- In this implemented model, **normative trajectories have higher `C_ent` variance than anxious trajectories**.

### 2. ~47% longer residence time in high-entropy hubs for anxious trajectories

Status: **partially confirmed in direction, not in magnitude**

- Poster claim: `~47%` longer
- Observed anxious increase over normative: `31.08%`
- 95% bootstrap CI: `[28.53%, 33.55%]`
- Mann-Whitney p-value: effectively `0`

Interpretation:

- The anxious regime does spend longer in high-entropy hubs than the normative regime.
- The effect is materially smaller than the poster projection.

### 3. Lower Hurst exponent for pathological regimes

Status: **not confirmed as a general statement**

Observed mean Hurst exponents:

- Normative: `0.7499`
- Anxious: `0.8622`
- Ruminative: `0.4803`
- Psychotic: `0.8314`

Interpretation:

- Only the **ruminative** regime is clearly lower than normative.
- Both **anxious** and **psychotic** are higher than normative in the current implementation.
- The broad abstract statement "pathological regimes have lower H" should therefore be revised.

### 4. Phase transition at `⟨k⟩²/N ≈ 2.5`

Status: **not directly supported by the current validated reference artifact used here**

- Current reference curve loaded from `results/experiments/phase_transition_pure_julia.json`
- Interpolated critical point from that artifact: `η_c ≈ 2.94`
- SWOW-EN substrate used in CPC pipeline: `η ≈ 0.0195`

Interpretation:

- The CPC simulations run on a graph that is **far below** the generic geometric transition.
- The phrase "SWOW-EN is near the 2.5 transition" would be false for this pipeline.
- The honest formulation is:
  - the repository contains a generic ORC phase-transition reference on random regular graphs,
  - the sparse SWOW-EN substrate is deeply subcritical and hyperbolic,
  - regime differences arise from the trajectory model on that substrate, not from SWOW-EN sitting near `η_c`.

## Required Revisions To The Poster Narrative

The full paper should not repeat the current poster claims verbatim. The validated paper-facing wording should instead reflect:

1. `C_ent` variance separates regimes, but the normative condition is more variable than the anxious condition in the present model.
2. Anxious trajectories do show elevated residence in high-entropy hubs, but the observed increase is `~31%`, not `~47%`.
3. Hurst differences are heterogeneous across pathology-like regimes rather than uniformly lower.
4. The generic phase-transition reference belongs to the repository's random-regular ORC program, while SWOW-EN itself remains deep in the hyperbolic subcritical regime.

## Bottom Line

The CPC 2026 extension is now reproducible and yields coherent, nontrivial results, but the accepted poster abstract should be treated as a projection rather than a confirmed quantitative summary.

The full paper can proceed, provided it reports the observed values above and explicitly corrects the poster-era overclaims.
